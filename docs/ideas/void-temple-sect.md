## History

### Before the mandate

The Void Temple predates every other Heartlands great sect in its **current duty**. They were not founded to teach the jianghu — they were founded to **hold something**.

A **pre-heaven cultivator** — unbound by heaven’s rules, possibly a **different cultivation track** *(parked)* — could not be destroyed. Killing failed. Banishment failed. The one who would become the **Void Seeker** bound him in a **void pocket**: **Void Prison**.

The prisoner, with black humor, named it **Little Heaven** (小天堂). The name stuck in inner court.

### Dao Wars — neutral because the seal mattered more

While Sword capitulated, Lotus allied, and Phoenix broke, the Temple **refused the board**. Charter wars were noise; **rupturing pre-heaven seals** was the real catastrophe.

They signed the **Archive Oath** with the rising mandate:

- **Void Prison** and gate network stay sovereign.
- Temple catalogs sealed sites tied to the lock.
- No faction may force unsealing without Temple witness.

They were **neutral late** — not from weakness, from **priority**.

### After the mandate — the Seeker left

The prison held. Maintenance did not end. The Void Seeker — already **Immortal** — could not **finish** the prisoner. He laid the lock, trained the **Watch**, and entered the **outer void** to find a true end: unmake, kill, or seal that does not require eternal upkeep.

He has **not** retired. He **returns through gates** when the seal strains. Some Watch elders say he is dead beyond the sky; gate signatures say otherwise.

### Now

- **Oldest, most secluded** — sought out, not wandered into.
- **Hanging Star** floats as known heart; **Little Heaven** is the secret core.
- **Branches** are gate shrines — one city circle, one warden team, minimal public face.
- **Lotus** trades; **Sword** duels; **Void** answers when stars go wrong or a site wakes.

## The Void Seeker (寻虚仙) — sect immortal

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Epithet** | **The Void Seeker** (寻虚仙) — upper-echelon name; **not** his true name |
| **True name** | Unknown / unspoken |
| **Realm** | **Immortal** — restricted; void-aligned |
| **Role** | Binder of Void Prison; **hunter in the outer void** for a final solution |
| **Where** | **Beyond the firmament** — returns via **Void Gates** unpredictably |
| **Knowable mystery** | Everyone agrees he **was** real; debate **what** he is now |

### Who he is

He built what the continent could not finish. He does not administer Hanging Star — the **Watch** and **duty patriarch** do. His work is **outside**.

When he returns:

- Seal renewal, script correction, warden briefings.
- Rare audiences for those the Watch vouches for.
- Gate combat if something **leaks**.

**Not:** a sleeping god, a hidden spider, a lecture-hall patriarch. **Is:** the absent warden on an eternal errand.

### Void-aligned immortal play *(fiction)*

- Transit between **gate nodes** without crossing mortal distance.
- Read **firmament stress** — when Little Heaven strains.
- Touch **outer-void qi** without instant dao fracture — what lineage trains toward.

Relative to Sword Immortal: may be **weaker in open duel**; **stranger** in where and when he acts.

## Geography

### Hanging Star Observatory (悬空星观) — outer heart

Floating installation below the firmament — star charts, silence, training for VR pipeline. **Real** sect life: scribes, wardens, rotating patriarch’s office.

**Not** where the prisoner lives.

### Void Temple Steps — ground anchor

Map node `void_temple_steps`: *“Star charts line the ascending stairs; silence is the first lesson.”* Pilgrim station — ascent to Hanging Star or gate transfer. Most visitors **stop here** unless admitted.

### Void Prison / Little Heaven (虚空狱 · 小天堂) — true core

Pocket realm — pre-heaven prison tech, suppression arrays, watch rotation. ~60–70% of serious sect effort *(fiction)*. Prisoner within.

**Rumor:** “Void Temple has a hidden realm.” **True** — Little Heaven is not marketed.

### Void Gates — branch network

| Layer | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Confirmed** | One gate circle per **major city** (Longcheng, Tide Harbor, …) — shrine, not campus |
| **Rumored** | Military gates, sealed-site gates, one-use passages, Seeker-only keys |
| **Use** | Warden deployment, seal-node service, **Seeker return** — not public transport |
| **Deterrent** | Nobody knows full gate map; strike one branch → wardens may exit another |

### Sealed-site authority

Best **read** on pre-heaven sites and whether a dig threatens the **continental lock**. Friendly rep → `heartlands_root` clue chain.
